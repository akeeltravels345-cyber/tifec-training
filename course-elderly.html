Caring for the Elderly

Led by Georgia Isaacs, Allied Health Professional with 8+ years of experience and 3+ years teaching at university level.

Caring for an older person well takes more than kindness. It takes skill. Compassionate, competent care starts with the right knowledge, and this hands-on course covers everything from the aging process to essential care procedures. You'll gain the clinical awareness and the confidence to act, so you can support elderly clients with dignity and genuine care. CPR training is included.

Who it's for

Family caregivers, community health workers, and anyone stepping into an entry-level care role. All you need is an open mind and basic English.

What you'll learn

By the end of this course you'll be able to:

  • Define the caregiver role and understand the aging process
  • Communicate effectively with elderly clients and their families
  • Identify the physical, psychological, and psychosocial changes of aging
  • Recognise elder abuse and support clients through grief and loss
  • Understand the rights and basic needs of the elderly
  • Monitor vital signs: blood pressure, pulse, temperature, and blood sugar
  • Perform essential care procedures safely and competently
  • Administer CPR and respond in basic emergency situations

Course content

Foundational topics

  • Orientation to aging
  • Communication skills
  • The role of family in elder care
  • The role of the caregiver
  • Work ethics in care settings
  • Services for the elderly
  • Physical changes of aging
  • Psychological & psychosocial changes
  • Common problems of aging
  • Elder abuse awareness
  • Grief & loss
  • Rights of the elderly

Hands-on clinical skills

  • Blood pressure, pulse, respiration, and temperature
  • Blood sugar monitoring
  • Bed-making, bed bath, and mouth care
  • Urinal/bedpan use and catheter care
  • Wound care and tube feeding
  • Medication administration
  • Nutrition and stimulation exercises
  • Recording, reporting, and evaluating care
  • CPR
